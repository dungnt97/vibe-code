## What is DeFi?

Decentralized Finance, or DeFi, refers to a broad category of financial applications built on blockchain networks. These protocols aim to recreate and improve upon traditional financial services without the need for centralized intermediaries.

## Core DeFi Concepts

### Lending and Borrowing

DeFi lending protocols allow users to lend their crypto assets and earn interest, or borrow assets by providing collateral. Popular platforms include Aave and Compound.

### Automated Market Makers (AMMs)

AMMs are a type of decentralized exchange that uses mathematical formulas to price assets. Instead of traditional order books, AMMs use liquidity pools funded by users who earn fees in return.

### Yield Farming

Yield farming involves strategically moving assets between different DeFi protocols to maximize returns. While potentially lucrative, it comes with significant risks including smart contract vulnerabilities and impermanent loss.

## Getting Started Safely

1. **Start small** - Begin with well-established protocols
2. **Do your research** - Understand the risks before committing funds
3. **Use hardware wallets** - Keep your private keys secure
4. **Diversify** - Don't put all your assets in one protocol
